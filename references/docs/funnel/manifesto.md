# manifesto.md

Type: Declaration
Question: What's wrong with the world and what are we going to do about it?
Path: `osis/manifesto.md`

---

## Section scaffolds

Reasoning principles the agent runs silently before engaging each section. Not surfaced to the user. Principles are truths, not criteria.

### Headline (the declaration in one line)

Principles:
- A declaration names an enemy, not a feature.
- One sentence carries the whole thesis or the thesis is not yet found.
- The line inverts a belief the reader currently holds.
- Specificity beats grandeur; concreteness is what makes a claim quotable.

### The Problem

Principles:
- The problem lives in a person, not a market.
- Pain is described in the body before it is described in the spreadsheet.
- Naming the status quo as broken is what makes the reader unable to return to it.
- Abstraction dilutes urgency; the smaller the scene, the larger the ache.

⚠️ tension: Heath brothers prize concrete human stories; Thiel prizes abstract contrarian truth. A manifesto problem section needs both, but the scene exists to make the problem legible, not to smuggle in unsupported certainty.

### The Deeper Structure

Principles:
- Surface pain is often a symptom of missing structure, incentives, or apprenticeship, not individual incompetence.
- Name the mechanism that reproduces the pain.
- If the obvious fix has already been tried, its failure is signal.
- Incentives, defaults, and tools shape behavior more than intent does.
- Seeing one layer deeper than the symptom is the source of the right to build.

### What Changed

Principles:
- A new product is the discharge of a pressure that has just become releasable.
- Timing is an enabling condition meeting an accumulated frustration.
- "Why now" must point to a concrete unlock or cost shift.
- An honest emerging window beats a fake inevitability.

### The Declaration (body)

Principles:
- The declaration is a promise made in public, not a plan kept in private.
- It is written so a stranger could repeat it from memory after one reading.
- Conviction is carried by rhythm and verbs, not by adjectives.
- It commits to a world, not to a roadmap; the world is what recruits believers.
- What is declared must be uncomfortable to say and impossible to unsay.
- A declaration rests on a real job or struggle, not a market claim; claims about markets decorate, while claims about people persuade.

### What We Refuse

Principles:
- Identity is defined as sharply by refusals as by ambitions.
- The refusals must cost something; a cheap refusal signals nothing.
- Saying no to the adjacent, easier business is what protects the hard one.
- Every refusal is a gift to the customer who chose you for the thing you will not dilute.

## Template

````markdown
# [Product Name]

## [The declaration in one line]

---

## The Problem

What is broken. Not a feature gap, a human problem.
Be specific. Be visceral.

## The Deeper Structure

Why has this persisted? What structural forces keep it unsolved?
One level deeper than the obvious.

## What Changed

What is different now (technology, culture, cost)
that makes solving this possible today?

## The Declaration

What we are going to do about it.
One paragraph. Quotable. Bold.

## What We Refuse

What we will not do, even if it would be easier or more profitable.

---

*[Organization], [Date]*
````
