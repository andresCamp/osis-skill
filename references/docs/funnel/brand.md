# brand.md

Type: Expression
Question: How does this product express itself?
Path: `osis/brand.md`

---

## Section scaffolds

Reasoning principles the agent runs silently before engaging each section. Not surfaced to the user. Principles are truths, not criteria.

### Identity

Principles:
- A product's identity is the shortest true sentence that makes its reason for existing legible to a stranger.
- Identity names what kind of thing the product is before it names what the product does.
- Identity held by the builder is upstream of identity perceived by the user; drift between the two is the brand's real debt.
- Identity is decided by subtraction; what the product refuses to be is as load-bearing as what it claims to be.
- Brand is a meaning system, not a style layer; style without meaning produces polish that does not compound.

### Voice

Principles:
- Voice is the invariant personality across surfaces; tone is its modulation by context.
- Voice is defined by pairing each trait with the adjacent trait it is not, so writers know where the edge is.
- Voice lives in word choice, sentence rhythm, and what goes unsaid, not in taglines or adjectives about the brand.
- Consistency across every touchpoint is what makes voice feel like a person rather than a committee.
- Voice shows up most at the edges: errors, empty states, invoices, onboarding, the unsexy surfaces where brand leaks.

⚠️ tension: canonical guides treat voice as stable and codified; greenfield operators treat voice as a founder's point of view that sharpens faster by shipping than by documenting.

### Positioning

Principles:
- Positioning is a stake against the alternatives the user is actually considering, not against the full market.
- The relevant alternatives include the status quo, a spreadsheet, and doing nothing, not only named competitors.
- A position is owned in the user's head, not on the product's website; it exists only to the extent it displaces another idea.
- Differentiation without a point of view on the market degrades into feature lists.

⚠️ tension: classical positioning fights for a seat in an existing category; category-design thinking argues the stronger move is naming a new category the product is the only entrant in. Choose on purpose.

### Naming and Language

Principles:
- Naming is framing: the chosen word imports the frame, the prior associations, and the opposition it implies.
- A product owns a word by using it with a single meaning everywhere and refusing its competitor's vocabulary.
- Internal terms leak into user surfaces; the language used in engineering becomes the language users inherit.
- Jargon borrowed from an adjacent category inherits that category's ceiling and constraints.
- Banned terms are as load-bearing as preferred terms, because consistency is produced by exclusion.

For [audience]
